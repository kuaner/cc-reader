## Design Context

### Users
Developers who use Claude Code and Codex daily. They browse session history to debug, review, and resume work. Context: at their desk, often late at night, after a long coding session. They value speed and clarity over decoration.

### Brand Personality
**Quiet and technical.** The app feels like a well-worn lab notebook — warm but restrained, precise but not sterile. It doesn't announce itself loudly; it earns trust through craft. Three words: *quiet, precise, analog*.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark. Matches the app's native dark mode and the developer's late-night context.
- **Palette**: The app's "Session Ledger" system — warm stone grays (#1a1614 base), amber accents (#c4956a), muted text (#a89888). Derived from the Ledger CSS theme. Tinted neutrals, never pure black or white.
- **Typography**: System fonts (San Francisco via -apple-system). The app uses SF natively; the landing page should match. No decorative web fonts — the voice is technical, not editorial.
- **Tone**: Understated. No glowing effects, no gradient text, no hero animations. Let the screenshot and feature descriptions carry the weight.
- **Anti-reference**: Not a SaaS landing page. No "supercharge your workflow" copy. No purple gradients. No oversized emoji icons.

### Design Principles
1. **Restraint over spectacle** — every element earns its place. If removing something doesn't hurt, remove it.
2. **Match the app** — the landing page should feel like a natural extension of the macOS app, not a separate marketing artifact.
3. **Information density** — developers scan, don't read. Compact layouts with clear hierarchy beat spacious layouts with filler.
4. **Warm but not cozy** — the amber palette adds warmth, but the layout and voice stay precise and technical.
5. **System-native feel** — use native-feeling patterns: SF symbols style, macOS-like spacing, subtle borders over shadows.
